My Story

I've been exactly where you are.

I remember the first time. Waiting room. White lights. My three-year-old — no words, not looking at anyone — and me, trying to smile like I understood what was happening.

Two years later, I lived it again. Same room. Same diagnosis. This time it was my younger daughter. Two non-verbal girls. One mom trying not to break.

The next ten years I spent in rooms like those — no longer as the mom who was lost. As the mom who took notes. The therapist would speak to my daughter, and I'd write it down. The speech pathologist would introduce a card, and I'd learn how to use it. The special educator would build a routine, and I'd run it at home that night.

That's how I learned First & Then. That's how I learned visual schedules. That's how I learned token systems. Not from a book — from the specialists who, week after week, trained my daughters, and without knowing it, trained me too.

Today I want to give you what took me a decade to learn — in Spanish, from my kitchen table, mom to mom. You are not alone in this. And you don't have to wait another ten years to start.

Session Topics

Choose the topic your child needs today.

Each session covers a single topic and one specific visual support. You don't have to take them all — start with the one your family needs now.

First & Then

The simplest and most powerful visual support for teaching transitions: first what needs to happen, then what your child wants. Reduces frustration and meltdowns immediately.

Visual Schedule

Your child's entire day in images — from breakfast to bedtime. Provides structure, predictability and autonomy. Learn to build it, adjust it, and keep it alive at home.

Token System

How to motivate positive behaviors with a visual token system — no bribes, no yelling, no exhaustion. A concrete tool your child understands even if they don't speak.

Functional Visual Supports

Visual vocabulary, communication boards, choice cards and more. How to introduce them, prepare them, and implement them so your child can express themselves — even without words.
